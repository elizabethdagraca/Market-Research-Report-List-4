<p><p>Insulin preparation market is segmented into fast-acting, intermediate-acting, and long-acting types. Fast-acting insulin is designed to start working quickly after injection, typically reaching peak effectiveness within 30 minutes to an hour. Intermediate-acting insulin takes longer to start working but lasts longer in the body, typically peaking after 2-4 hours. Long-acting insulin provides a steady level of insulin in the body for an extended period, usually lasting up to 24 hours. This segmentation caters to different patient needs and preferences for managing diabetes.</p></p>
