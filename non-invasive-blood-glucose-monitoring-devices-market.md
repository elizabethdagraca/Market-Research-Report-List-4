<p><p>The non-invasive blood glucose monitoring devices market comprises various techniques. Mid/Near Infrared (MIR/NIR) spectroscopy utilizes light absorption patterns to estimate glucose levels. Raman spectroscopy analyzes molecular vibrations for glucose detection. Occlusion spectroscopy examines changes in light transmission during blood flow interruption. Optical coherence tomography provides detailed tissue imaging. Photoacoustic spectroscopy combines sound and light to measure glucose. Electromagnetic methods use electromagnetic fields for monitoring, while fluorescence techniques detect glucose reactivity. Each technology offers unique advantages in glucose management without skin penetration.</p></p>
