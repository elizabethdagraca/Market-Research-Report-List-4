<p><ul><li>Card Emulation</li><li>Reader Emulation</li><li>Peer-to-peer</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Near Field Communication (NFC) technology is utilized in three main market types: Card Emulation, Reader Emulation, and Peer-to-peer. In Card Emulation, NFC-enabled devices act as a virtual smart card, allowing users to make secure payments and access transportation systems. Reader Emulation enables devices to read NFC tags or cards, providing information or triggering actions. Peer-to-peer allows two NFC-enabled devices to communicate with each other, exchanging data such as photos, videos, or contact information wirelessly. Each market type serves different purposes and applications in the NFC ecosystem.</p></p>
