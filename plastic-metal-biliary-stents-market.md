<p><p>Plastic biliary stents are typically made of materials such as polyethylene or Teflon, and are used for short-term drainage of biliary strictures. They are flexible and easy to insert, but may not provide long-term support. On the other hand, metal biliary stents, often made of stainless steel or nitinol, are used for longer-term relief of biliary obstructions. They are more durable and provide better support for the bile duct, but may require additional procedures for removal. Both types of stents play crucial roles in managing various biliary disorders.</p></p>
