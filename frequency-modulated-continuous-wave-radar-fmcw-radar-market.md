<p><strong>The Frequency-Modulated Continuous-Wave Radar (FMCW Radar) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Sawtooth Modulation</li><li>Triangular Modulation</li><li>Square-wave Modulation</li><li>Stepped Modulation</li><li>Sinusoidal Modulation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Frequency-Modulated Continuous-Wave Radar (FMCW Radar) utilizes different modulation techniques for signal processing. Sawtooth modulation involves linearly increasing frequency, triangular modulation uses a linear frequency sweep in both directions, square-wave modulation alternates between high and low frequencies, stepped modulation shifts frequencies in discrete steps, and sinusoidal modulation provides a continuous frequency sweep. Each type of modulation offers unique advantages and is used in various applications such as automotive radar, industrial sensing, and defense systems.</p></p>
