<p><p>Microfibrillar Cellulose (MFC) is produced through various methods such as Acid Hydrolysis, Enzymatic Hydrolysis, and Electrospinning. Acid Hydrolysis method involves breaking down cellulose fibers using strong acids, while Enzymatic Hydrolysis method uses enzymes to achieve the same result in a more eco-friendly way. Electrospinning market produces MFC through an electrostatic spinning process. Each method has its own advantages and applications, contributing to the diverse uses of microfibrillar cellulose in industries such as food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
