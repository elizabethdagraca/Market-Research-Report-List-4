<p><ul><li>Coulomb Type Electrostatic Chuck</li><li>Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type Electrostatic Chuck</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bipolar electrostatic chucks are used in semiconductor manufacturing to hold substrates in place during the fabrication process. There are two main types of bipolar electrostatic chucks: Coulomb Type Electrostatic Chuck and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) Type Electrostatic Chuck. The Coulomb type chuck uses electrostatic attraction to hold the substrate in place, while the JR type chuck utilizes a combination of electrostatic forces and mechanical clamping. Both types of chucks offer precise and reliable substrate holding capabilities, crucial for the success of semiconductor manufacturing processes.</p></p>
